--- source: 1 RCNY §103-14 & Admin. Code §28-320.3 — How the emissions limit is computed (ESPM factors × area) | url: https://www.nyc.gov/assets/buildings/rules/1_RCNY_103-14.pdf ---
A covered building's annual building emissions limit is the sum, across the building's space uses, of an emissions-intensity factor for each use multiplied by the gross floor area of that use. Each space is classified by its EnergyStar Portfolio Manager (ESPM) property type, and the Department of Buildings rule 1 RCNY §103-14 assigns each ESPM property type an emissions factor in tCO2e per square foot. For the 2024-2029 compliance period, for example, Multifamily Housing has a factor of 0.00675 tCO2e/sf, Office 0.00758, and Hospital 0.02381. Limit = Σ (gross floor area of each ESPM property type × that type's emissions factor). The factors decline for the 2030-2034 period, tightening the limit. Gross floor area is defined in §103-14 as the total area of all floors measured to the exterior of the enclosing walls, including basements, mechanical rooms, and interior parking, and must be determined by a registered design professional.

A building's annual greenhouse-gas emissions are computed by multiplying each energy source's annual consumption by a source-specific GHG emissions coefficient and summing across all sources: annual emissions = Σ (annual use of each fuel × that fuel's emissions coefficient). Compliance is met when the GHG emissions limit is greater than or equal to the building's annual GHG emissions. The electricity coefficient declines over time as the New York grid decarbonizes, while the coefficients for natural gas and fuel oil stay effectively constant. As a result, electrifying heating and hot water (beneficial electrification — replacing fossil-fuel systems with efficient electric heat pumps) becomes disproportionately valuable for meeting the stricter limits of the 2030-2034 and later compliance periods.
