--- source: NYC Local Law 97 — Compliance periods and the tightening 2030 emissions cap | url: https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/codes/local-law-97.page ---
Local Law 97 sets annual greenhouse-gas emissions limits that apply in successive compliance periods. The first compliance period runs from calendar year 2024 through 2029 (CP1). The second compliance period runs from 2030 through 2034 (CP2), with substantially stricter emissions limits — the 2030 cap is sharply lower than the 2024-2029 cap, which is why owners refer to a "2030 cliff." Later periods cover 2035-2039 and 2040-2049, with limits set on a trajectory toward citywide carbon neutrality by 2050. Covered-building owners must submit an annual building emissions report to the Department of Buildings by May 1 of each year; the report for the first compliance calendar year, 2024, was due by May 1, 2025. A building can be compliant in 2024-2029 yet fall out of compliance in 2030-2034 because the limit drops while the building's emissions stay the same.
