--- source: NYC Administrative Code §28-320.3.10 — Good-faith effort & penalty mitigation | url: https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/codes/local-law-97.page ---
A "good-faith effort" under Local Law 97 refers to documented steps an owner takes toward compliance that the Department of Buildings may consider when assessing penalties for the 2024-2029 period. The Department's rules allow mitigated penalties for owners who demonstrate a good-faith effort to comply rather than ignoring the law. Examples of good-faith-effort evidence include having filed required benchmarking (Local Law 84) and energy-audit and retro-commissioning reports (Local Law 87), applying for available financing and incentives, beginning the design or installation of decarbonization and electrification work, submitting a decarbonization plan, and being on a credible path toward the building's emissions limit. A good-faith effort does not erase or reduce the building's emissions limit itself, but it can serve as the basis for a reduced penalty where the owner is making real, documented progress toward compliance.
