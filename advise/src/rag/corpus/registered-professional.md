The official Local Law 97 building emissions report must be prepared and certified by a registered design professional — a New York State licensed professional engineer (PE) or registered architect (RA). The registered design professional is responsible for determining the building's gross floor area, classifying each space into the correct EnergyStar Portfolio Manager (ESPM) property type, and certifying the annual emissions and emissions-limit calculation. Estimates produced by third-party planning tools are useful for budgeting and decision-making but are not a substitute for the certified report the Department of Buildings requires. Owners must keep the documentation used to prepare each report for at least six years and submit it on request.
