--- source: NYC Administrative Code §28-320.1 — Covered-building threshold (over 25,000 sq ft) | url: https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/codes/local-law-97.page ---
Local Law 97 applies to "covered buildings." As defined in NYC Administrative Code Article 320 and based on the records of the Department of Finance, a covered building is (i) a building that exceeds 25,000 gross square feet (about 2,322.5 m²), (ii) two or more buildings on the same tax lot that together exceed 50,000 gross square feet, or (iii) two or more buildings held in the condominium form of ownership governed by the same board of managers that together exceed 50,000 gross square feet. Where buildings on one tax lot together exceed 50,000 square feet, each building on the lot over 25,000 square feet is covered. Several categories are excepted, including certain industrial facilities used to generate electric power or steam, city buildings, NYCHA housing, certain rent-regulated accommodations, and houses of worship. The Department of Buildings publishes a Covered Buildings List by borough-block-lot (BBL) for reference; presence or absence on the list does not by itself determine compliance obligations.
