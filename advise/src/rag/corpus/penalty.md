--- source: NYC Administrative Code §28-320.6 — Civil penalty for exceeding the emissions limit | url: https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/codes/local-law-97.page ---
Under Local Law 97 (NYC Administrative Code Article 320), an owner of a covered building whose annual building emissions exceed its annual building emissions limit is liable for a civil penalty. The penalty is assessed annually and equals the amount by which the building's reported annual emissions exceed its calculated emissions limit, in metric tons of carbon dioxide equivalent (tCO2e), multiplied by $268 per ton. In formula form: penalty = (annual GHG emissions − GHG emissions limit) × $268 per tCO2e. A building whose emissions are at or below its limit owes no penalty. The $268-per-ton rate was set by the Department of Buildings rule and applies for excess emissions in each compliance period. Penalties for the first compliance period (calendar years 2024 through 2029) begin to accrue based on the building emissions reports owners must file each year.
