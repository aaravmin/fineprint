--- source: NYC Administrative Code §28-321 (Article 321) — Affordable-housing & rent-regulated alternative compliance pathway | url: https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/codes/local-law-97.page ---
Article 321 (NYC Administrative Code §28-321) is the alternative, lower-cost compliance pathway for the affordable-housing and rent-regulated bucket of Local Law 97. It covers buildings where more than 35% of the dwelling units are rent-regulated, income-restricted affordable housing such as HDFC and limited-profit (Mitchell-Lama) housing, and houses of worship. A building that qualifies for the Article 321 affordable-housing pathway does not have to meet the numeric annual emissions cap of Article 320 and does not incur the $268-per-excess-ton penalty. Instead it must either implement all applicable prescribed energy-conservation measures or, alternatively, demonstrate that it already meets the 2030 (Article 320) annual emissions limit. Owners on this pathway file a one-time combined building emissions report rather than annual reports. To qualify for the affordable-housing prescriptive pathway you must confirm the building's rent-regulation or affordability status, which is not always provable from public datasets and must be certified by the owner.

--- source: 1 RCNY §103-14 & Article 321 — Prescribed energy-conservation measures and the early-2030 option | url: https://www.nyc.gov/site/buildings/codes/local-law-97.page ---
A building on the Article 321 affordable-housing pathway complies by implementing the prescribed list of energy-conservation measures applicable to it. The prescribed measures include adjusting temperature set points to prevent overheating, repairing heating-system leaks, maintaining heating-system components, adding radiator or individual temperature controls, insulating exposed heating and hot-water pipes, insulating hot-water storage tanks, installing indoor and outdoor temperature sensors, repairing or replacing steam traps, installing or repairing master steam venting, upgrading lighting to LED, and weatherizing the building envelope. As an alternative to the prescriptive measures, an Article 321 building may instead meet the 2030-2034 (Article 320) annual emissions limit early — the performance option — and be treated as compliant. Deadlines for the income-restricted and rent-regulated categories are extended relative to the standard 2024 start: for example, the initial report for buildings with fewer than 35% rent-regulated units is due May 1, 2027, and certain income-restricted and limited-profit housing companies have until May 1, 2036.
